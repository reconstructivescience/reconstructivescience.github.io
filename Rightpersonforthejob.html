Video Summary

There are many qualities that a reconstructive scientist should have and this video names just a few of them.

What Qualities Should I Have?

There are many important aspects to a reconstructive scientist, perhaps the most important being that you understand all the risks that any surgery carries, as well as recognising the health and safety rules. Here are a few more important qualities:
  • Be able to analyse data – scientific, clinical and technical.
  • Be able to design and sculpt complex prosthetics.
  • Have an understanding of technology and be able to embrace any new advancements.
  • Be able to manage treatment programmes for referred patients appropriately.
  • Communicate with patients in a reassuring and social way.
  • Be calm, as you will often be working in the theatre under pressure.
