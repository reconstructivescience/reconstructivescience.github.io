Day In The Life

Video Summary

In this video, you will learn some of the different roles that make up the job of a reconstructive scientist.

Patients

Whilst working, you will experience patients requiring surgery for many reasons such as malformation from birth (e.g. born without a jaw), effects of a disease (e.g. cancer), or trauma (e.g. a car accident). You could be working on any part of the body, but most commonly it will be the jaw, face and the skull.

General Information

  • You will design, construct and apply medical devices (such as prosthetics or titanium skull plates) for patients; these have to be custom-made and you must remember each job is different.
  • During an operation, you are required to attend theatre to give advice on the positioning of any implants.
  • You will work in either a maxillofacial or plastic surgery department within an NHS hospital.
  • Nose surgeons, dental technologists and maxillofacial surgeons may form part of your team.
