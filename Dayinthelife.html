Day In The Life

As a Reconstructive Scientist working as part of the NHS, you do a whole array of things at work each day. This can include:
  • Talking to patients
  • Making prosthetics
  • Measuring splints
  • Implanting prosthetics
  • Follow up appointments

Patients

Whilst working, you will experience patients requiring surgery because of reasons such as malformation from birth (e.g born without a jaw), effects of a disease (such as cancer), or trauma (e.g a car accident). You could be working on any part of the body, but most commonly the jaw, face and the skull.

General Information

  • You will design, construct and apply medical devices (such as prosthetics or titanium skull plates) for patients; these have to be custom-made and must keep in mind that no job is the same.
  • During an operation, you are required to attend theatre to give advice on the positioning of any implants.
  • You will work in a maxillofacial or plastic surgery department within an NHS hospital.
  • Nose surgeons, dental technologists and maxillofacial surgeons may form part of your team.
