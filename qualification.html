Qualifications

Reconstructive science goes hand in hand with maxilofacial science. To apply for the training programme you must have:
  • Five GCSEs grade 4–9, including all three sciences
  • Three high-grade A-levels
  • A degree in dental technology
Then you should work as a Dental Technologist for a few years. Finally then can you apply for the NHS Reconstructive Scientist!

To become a reconstructive scientist, you’ll need five GCSE grades, which must be grade 4 – 9 and must include all of the three sciences (biology, chemistry and physics). You must take 3 A-levels and get decent grades to be able to apply for an approved degree in dental technology at university. It is of primary importance that you check university entry requirements as these may differ between institutions. Then you can apply for the NHS Scientist Training Programme, which means extra training to specialise and gain an MSc in reconstructive science, but only after you have worked as a dental technologist.
